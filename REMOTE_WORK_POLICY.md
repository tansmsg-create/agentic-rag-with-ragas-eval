# Remote Work and Flexible Work Arrangement Policy

**1. Introduction**

This Policy sets out how [Fictional IT Company Name] ("the Company") manages remote work and other flexible work arrangements ("FWAs"), in line with Singapore's Tripartite Guidelines on Flexible Work Arrangement Requests ("TG-FWAR"). All employees and people managers are expected to read, understand, and comply with this Policy.

**2. Types of Flexible Work Arrangements**

The Company recognises three broad categories of FWA, which may be combined where appropriate:

*   **Flexi-Place:** Working from a location other than the primary office, including work-from-home arrangements.
*   **Flexi-Time:** Staggered working hours or compressed work weeks.
*   **Flexi-Load:** Part-time work or job-sharing arrangements.

**3. Eligibility**

Employees who have completed their probation period may submit a formal FWA request. Managers and executives not otherwise covered by the Employment Act are equally entitled to submit a request under this Policy.

**4. Submitting a Request**

Formal FWA requests should be submitted in writing to the employee's direct manager and HR, and should include:

*   The type of arrangement requested (Flexi-Place, Flexi-Time, Flexi-Load, or a combination).
*   The proposed start date and expected duration.
*   How the employee intends to meet their work obligations under the proposed arrangement.

**5. Considering Requests**

Requests are reviewed by the employee's direct manager in consultation with HR, and assessed fairly on business grounds. Acceptable reasons for declining a request include the role requiring physical presence, shift or coverage requirements, and client-facing obligations. Requests may not be declined on the basis of management bias, organisational tradition, or a general preference for physical visibility.

The Company will communicate a written decision to the employee within two months of receiving a formal request.

**6. Remote Work Expectations**

Employees approved for remote or hybrid work are expected to:

*   Be contactable and available during agreed core working hours.
*   Maintain a secure and suitable home working environment, including compliance with the Company's IT Security Policy when accessing Company systems remotely.
*   Attend in-person meetings, team days, or client engagements as reasonably required, even under a Flexi-Place arrangement.
*   Report equipment issues or connectivity problems promptly to IT.

**7. Equipment and Expenses**

The Company will provide reasonable equipment (e.g. laptop, headset) required to work remotely. Any additional equipment or expense claims should be discussed with HR in advance.

**8. Review and Withdrawal**

Approved FWAs are reviewed periodically and may be adjusted or withdrawn where business needs change, with reasonable notice given to the employee.

**9. Reporting Concerns**

Employees who believe an FWA request has been handled unfairly may raise the matter with HR for review.
