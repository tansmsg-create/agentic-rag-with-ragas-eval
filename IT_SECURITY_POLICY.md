# IT Security Policy

**1. Introduction**

This IT Security Policy (the "Policy") sets out the minimum information security standards that all employees of [Fictional IT Company Name] ("the Company") must follow to protect Company systems, data, and networks. This Policy supports the Company's wider information security management approach and its obligations under the Data Protection Policy.

**2. Scope**

This Policy applies to all employees, contractors, and third parties who access Company IT systems, networks, devices, or data, whether on Company premises, remotely, or via personal devices used for work purposes.

**3. Access Control**

*   Access to Company systems and data is granted on a least-privilege, need-to-know basis.
*   Employees must not share login credentials, passwords, or access tokens with any other person.
*   Multi-factor authentication ("MFA") is required for access to email, cloud storage, and other systems designated by IT as sensitive.
*   Access rights are reviewed periodically and revoked promptly upon an employee's departure or change of role.

**4. Password and Authentication Standards**

*   Passwords must meet the minimum complexity standards set by IT and must not be reused across systems.
*   Employees must not use national identification numbers (such as NRIC) as a means of authentication, in line with the Company's Data Protection Policy.
*   Suspected compromise of a password or account must be reported to IT immediately.

**5. Device and Endpoint Security**

*   Company-issued devices must have up-to-date antivirus and endpoint protection software, and must not have security controls disabled.
*   Devices must be locked when unattended, and full-disk encryption must be enabled on all laptops.
*   Personal devices used to access Company systems ("BYOD") must meet minimum security standards set by IT, including screen lock and remote-wipe capability.

**6. Network and Remote Access**

*   Employees working remotely must connect to Company systems via approved secure methods (e.g. VPN or equivalent), particularly when using public or unsecured Wi-Fi networks.
*   Unauthorised installation of software, browser extensions, or hardware on Company devices is prohibited without IT approval.

**7. Data Handling and Classification**

*   Confidential and sensitive data (including personal data covered by the Data Protection Policy) must only be stored on approved Company systems, not on unauthorised personal cloud storage or removable media.
*   Data must be encrypted in transit and at rest where technically feasible, particularly for sensitive or confidential categories of information.

**8. Incident Reporting**

*   Any suspected security incident — including phishing attempts, malware infection, lost or stolen devices, or unauthorised access — must be reported to IT immediately.
*   Employees must not attempt to investigate or remediate a suspected security incident themselves without IT guidance, to preserve evidence and avoid making the situation worse.

**9. Acceptable Use**

This Policy operates alongside the Company's Acceptable Use Policy, which governs the day-to-day use of IT resources for business purposes.

**10. Security Awareness Training**

All employees are required to complete IT security awareness training on induction and periodically thereafter, covering topics such as phishing recognition, password hygiene, and safe handling of confidential data.

**11. Consequences of Violations**

Violations of this Policy may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment, and may also expose the Company to regulatory, legal, or financial consequences.

*This document is a template and should be reviewed and customised by IT and legal counsel to meet specific organisational and jurisdictional requirements.*
