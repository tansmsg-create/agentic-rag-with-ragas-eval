# Acceptable Use Policy (AUP)

**1. Introduction**

This Acceptable Use Policy (AUP) outlines the terms and conditions for the use of [Fictional IT Company Name] ("the Company") information technology (IT) resources. These resources include, but are not limited to, computers, networks, internet access, email, software, and mobile devices provided by the Company. All employees are required to read, understand, and comply with this AUP.

**2. Purpose**

The purpose of this AUP is to protect the Company's IT assets, ensure network security, maintain productivity, and comply with legal and regulatory requirements.

**3. Acceptable Use**

*   **Business Purposes:** Company IT resources are primarily for business-related activities. Limited personal use may be permitted, provided it does not interfere with job duties, violate any other Company policies, or incur additional costs for the Company.
*   **Security:** Employees must take reasonable steps to protect Company IT resources from unauthorised access, damage, or misuse. This includes using strong passwords, locking workstations when unattended, and reporting suspicious activity.
*   **Software:** Only install software that is licensed and approved by the IT department. Unauthorised software may pose security risks.
*   **Network Usage:** Use the Company network responsibly. This includes refraining from excessive bandwidth consumption for non-business purposes.
*   **Email:** Company email is for business communications. Be mindful of the content you send and receive. Avoid sending inappropriate, offensive, or unsolicited commercial emails.

**4. Prohibited Use**

The following activities are strictly prohibited when using Company IT resources:

*   **Illegal Activities:** Engaging in any activity that violates local, state, federal, or international laws.
*   **Harassment or Discrimination:** Sending or accessing materials that are discriminatory, harassing, offensive, or violate the Company's EEO or Anti-Harassment policies.
*   **Unauthorised Access:** Attempting to access or gain unauthorised entry into any computer system, network, or data.
*   **Malicious Software:** Introducing viruses, worms, malware, or other harmful code to the Company network or systems.
*   **Copyright Infringement:** Downloading, sharing, or distributing copyrighted material without proper authorisation.
*   **Commercial Gain:** Using Company resources for personal commercial gain or in support of a personal business.
*   **Network Disruption:** Engaging in activities that disrupt network performance or availability for other users.
*   **Misrepresentation:** Impersonating others or misrepresenting your identity when using Company IT resources.
*   **Confidential Information:** Accessing, downloading, or sharing confidential Company or client information without proper authorisation.

**5. Monitoring and Privacy**

Employees should have no expectation of privacy when using Company IT resources. The Company reserves the right to monitor, access, and disclose any data or communications transmitted over its network or stored on its systems, to the extent permitted by law, for business purposes, security, or legal investigations.

**6. Enforcement**

Violations of this AUP may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment, and may also lead to legal action.

**7. Reporting**

Report any suspected violations of this AUP to the IT Department or your manager immediately.
