# Whistleblowing Policy

**1. Introduction**

This Whistleblowing Policy (the "Policy") sets out how [Fictional IT Company Name] ("the Company") enables employees and other stakeholders to raise genuine concerns about suspected misconduct, malpractice, or wrongdoing, without fear of retaliation. This Policy supports the standards set out in the Company's Code of Conduct.

**2. Purpose**

The Company is committed to conducting its business with honesty and integrity. This Policy provides a safe and confidential channel for individuals to report concerns at an early stage, and in the right way, so they can be properly investigated and addressed.

**3. What Can Be Reported**

Reportable concerns include, but are not limited to:

*   Financial malpractice, fraud, or irregularities in accounting or reporting.
*   Breaches of legal or regulatory obligations, including data protection and IT security requirements.
*   Corruption, bribery, or conflicts of interest.
*   Serious risks to health, safety, or the environment.
*   Harassment, discrimination, or other breaches of the Code of Conduct.
*   Concealment of any of the above.

**4. How to Raise a Concern**

Concerns may be raised through any of the following channels:

*   Directly to a manager or supervisor.
*   To HR or the designated whistleblowing contact.
*   Through the confidential whistleblowing reporting channel, where the reporter's identity is known only to the individuals responsible for handling the report.

Concerns may be raised anonymously; however, providing contact details, where the reporter is comfortable doing so, can help the Company investigate the matter more effectively.

**5. Protection Against Retaliation**

The Company prohibits retaliation of any kind against an individual who raises a concern in good faith, even where the concern is not ultimately substantiated. Retaliation includes, but is not limited to, dismissal, demotion, harassment, or any other detrimental treatment connected to the report. Any employee found to have retaliated against a whistleblower will be subject to disciplinary action.

**6. Good Faith Reporting**

This Policy protects individuals who raise concerns in good faith and with a reasonable belief that the information disclosed is substantially true. It does not protect malicious or knowingly false reports, which may themselves be treated as a disciplinary matter.

**7. Investigation Process**

*   All concerns raised under this Policy will be acknowledged and assessed promptly.
*   Investigations will be conducted objectively, proportionately, and with as much confidentiality as is practicable given the nature of the concern.
*   Where appropriate, the reporter will be updated on the outcome of the investigation, subject to confidentiality and legal constraints.

**8. External Reporting**

Where an individual reasonably believes internal reporting is not appropriate — for example, due to a conflict of interest at senior level — this Policy does not prevent them from raising the matter with an appropriate external regulator or authority.

**9. Responsibilities**

*   **Employees:** Report genuine concerns promptly and cooperate with any resulting investigation.
*   **Managers:** Take all concerns seriously, escalate appropriately, and never discourage or penalise reporting.
*   **HR / Designated Contact:** Ensure concerns are logged, investigated, and closed out appropriately, and that confidentiality is maintained throughout.
