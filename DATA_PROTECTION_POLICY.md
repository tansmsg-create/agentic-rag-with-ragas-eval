# Data Protection Policy

**1. Introduction**

This Data Protection Policy (the "Policy") sets out how [Fictional IT Company Name] ("the Company") collects, uses, discloses, and protects personal data in the course of its business, in accordance with Singapore's Personal Data Protection Act 2012 ("PDPA"). All employees are required to read, understand, and comply with this Policy.

**2. Scope**

This Policy applies to all personal data handled by the Company — whether belonging to employees, clients, vendors, or other individuals — and to all employees, contractors, and third parties who process personal data on the Company's behalf.

**3. Data Protection Officer**

The Company has appointed a Data Protection Officer ("DPO") responsible for ensuring compliance with the PDPA, handling data protection queries and complaints, and serving as the primary point of contact with the Personal Data Protection Commission ("PDPC"). Contact details for the DPO are available from HR.

**4. Core Obligations**

The Company observes the following PDPA obligations in all its data-handling activities:

*   **Consent Obligation:** Personal data is only collected, used, or disclosed with the individual's consent, or under a permitted exception recognised by the PDPA.
*   **Purpose Limitation Obligation:** Personal data is only collected, used, or disclosed for purposes that a reasonable person would consider appropriate in the circumstances, and which the individual has been informed of.
*   **Notification Obligation:** Individuals are notified of the purpose(s) for which their personal data will be collected, used, or disclosed, on or before such collection, use, or disclosure.
*   **Access and Correction Obligation:** Individuals may request access to their personal data held by the Company and request correction of any errors or omissions.
*   **Accuracy Obligation:** The Company makes reasonable efforts to ensure personal data is accurate and complete, particularly where it is likely to be used to make a decision affecting the individual.
*   **Protection Obligation:** The Company puts in place reasonable security arrangements to protect personal data against unauthorised access, collection, use, disclosure, or similar risks.
*   **Retention Limitation Obligation:** Personal data is not retained longer than necessary for legal or business purposes; retained data is disposed of securely.
*   **Transfer Limitation Obligation:** Personal data transferred outside Singapore is only transferred to recipients that provide a standard of protection comparable to the PDPA, typically through contractual safeguards.
*   **Accountability Obligation:** The Company maintains internal policies and practices necessary to meet its PDPA obligations, communicates them to employees, and can demonstrate compliance on request.

**5. Data Breach Notification**

Any suspected or confirmed data breach must be reported to the DPO immediately. Where a breach is assessed as notifiable — for example, where it results in significant harm to affected individuals or involves 500 or more individuals — the Company will notify the PDPC within the statutory timeframe and inform affected individuals as soon as practicable.

**6. Authentication Practices**

In line with regulatory guidance, the Company does not use NRIC numbers as a means of authenticating individuals and relies instead on alternative, more secure verification methods.

**7. Employee Responsibilities**

Employees who handle personal data in the course of their work must:

*   Only access personal data on a need-to-know basis.
*   Use personal data solely for authorised business purposes.
*   Report any suspected data breach or mishandling to the DPO without delay.
*   Complete data protection training as required by the Company.

**8. Consequences of Violations**

Failure to comply with this Policy may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment, and may also expose the Company and the individual to regulatory or legal consequences under the PDPA.
